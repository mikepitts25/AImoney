The Prompt Engineering Masterclass

The difference between mediocre AI output and incredible AI output is the prompt. This chapter teaches you the frameworks that professionals use to get consistent, high-quality results.

The CRISP Framework

Use this formula for every prompt:

  • Context — Give background information
  • Role — Tell the AI who to be
  • Instruction — State exactly what you want
  • Specifics — Add constraints, format, length
  • Polish — Request revisions and refinements

Example: Writing a Blog Post

Context: I run a personal finance blog for millennials. Role: You are an expert financial content writer with 10 years of experience. Instruction: Write a 1,200-word blog post about "5 Ways to Save $500 This Month." Specifics: Use a conversational tone, include specific dollar amounts, add subheadings for each tip, end with a call-to-action. Polish: After writing, review for clarity and add a compelling introduction hook.

Remember

Bad prompt = bad output. Good prompt = good output. Great prompt = output that makes clients say "wow." Every prompt in this guide follows the CRISP framework.

Choosing Your Writing Niche

The riches are in the niches. Generalist writers compete on price. Niche writers compete on value — and they charge 3-5x more.

Top 10 Profitable Writing Niches for AI Writers

  1. SaaS & Tech: Blog posts, case studies, whitepapers — $150-500/piece

  3. Health & Wellness: Evergreen content, huge market — $100-400/piece
  4. E-commerce: Product descriptions, email sequences — $50-200/piece

  6. Legal: Blog content for law firms — $200-500/piece

How to Choose

Pick a niche where you have some interest or experience. You don't need to be an expert — AI helps with research. But genuine curiosity makes the work sustainable and helps you spot quality issues in AI output.

Building a Portfolio in a Weekend

You can't land clients without samples. Here's how to create a professional portfolio in 48 hours using AI.

Saturday Afternoon: Draft all 3 articles using AI, then heavily edit and add your voice

Sunday Morning: Polish, proofread, and format each piece

Sunday Afternoon: Publish on Medium or your own simple website

AI Portfolio Prompt

I'm building a freelance writing portfolio in the [NICHE] space. Help me create a compelling 1,500-word article about [TOPIC]. Requirements: - Write in a professional but accessible tone - Include 5 subheadings with actionable advice - Add relevant statistics or data points - End with a strong conclusion and CTA - Format for web readability (short paragraphs, bullet points where appropriate) Important: This should read like expert-level content, not generic AI output. Add specific examples and unique insights.

Critical Step

Never publish raw AI output as your portfolio. Always rewrite 30-40% in your own voice, add personal insights, and fact-check every claim. Your portfolio should showcase your editing and thinking skills, not just AI's writing.

The AI-Assisted Writing Workflow

Here's the exact workflow that lets AI writers produce 3-5x more content without sacrificing quality.

The 5-Step AI Writing Process

  1. Research (10 min): Use AI to gather key points, stats, and angles on your topic
  2. Outline (5 min): Create a detailed outline with AI, then rearrange to your liking
  3. Draft (15 min): Generate the first draft section by section, not all at once
  4. Rewrite (20 min): Add your voice, fix awkward phrasing, inject original insights
  5. Polish (10 min): Use AI for grammar check, then do a final human read-through

Total time for a 1,500-word article: ~60 minutes vs. 3-4 hours without AI.

Section-by-Section Draft Prompt

Write the [SECTION NAME] section of my article about [TOPIC]. Context from my outline: - Main point: [POINT] - Key supporting details: [DETAILS] - Target length: 200-300 words Tone: [Professional/Conversational/Authoritative] Audience: [TARGET READER] Write this section so it flows naturally from the previous section about [PREV TOPIC].

Scaling to $5K/Month

Going from $0 to $1,000 is about landing clients. Going from $1,000 to $5,000 is about systems and pricing.

The $5K/Month Math

  • Option A: 5 clients × 4 posts/month × $250/post = $5,000
  • Option B: 3 retainer clients × $1,700/month = $5,100
  • Option C: 10 clients × 2 posts/month × $250/post = $5,000

How to Raise Your Rates

  1. Start at $100-150/post to build reviews and testimonials
  2. After 5 completed projects, raise to $200/post for new clients
  3. After 10 projects, move to $250-350/post and offer retainers
  4. At 20+ projects, charge $400+ or switch to value-based pricing

Scaling Secret

Retainers are the key to predictable income. Offer clients a discount for committing to a monthly package (e.g., 4 posts/month for $900 vs. $250/each). You get stability; they get a deal.

AI Image Generation Fundamentals

AI image generation has exploded. Tools like Midjourney, DALL-E, and Leonardo AI can create professional-quality images in seconds. Here's how to master them.

The Anatomy of a Great Image Prompt

Every effective image prompt has four parts:

  • Subject: What is the main focus? (e.g., "a golden retriever puppy")
  • Style: What art style? (e.g., "watercolor illustration," "minimalist vector")
  • Details: Colors, lighting, mood (e.g., "warm sunset lighting, soft pastels")
  • Technical: Aspect ratio, quality flags (e.g., "--ar 16:9 --q 2")

Example: T-Shirt Design Prompt

A minimalist line art illustration of a mountain landscape with a rising sun, clean vector style, single color (white on transparent background), suitable for t-shirt printing, simple and bold design, no background, centered composition --ar 1:1

Tool Recommendation

For print-on-demand designs, Leonardo AI's free tier is excellent for starting out. Midjourney ($10/mo) produces the most consistently sellable designs. Start free, upgrade when you're making sales.

Print-on-Demand Business Setup

Print-on-demand (POD) lets you sell physical products — t-shirts, mugs, posters, phone cases — without holding any inventory. You upload designs, customers order, and the POD company prints and ships.

Best POD Platforms

  • Redbubble: Easiest to start, built-in marketplace traffic
  • Merch by Amazon: Huge customer base, application required
  • Printful + Etsy: Best combo for brand control and organic traffic
  • TeePublic: Good secondary platform, easy uploads
  • Society6: Best for artistic/premium designs

The Launch Checklist

  1. Choose 3 niches to test (pets, hobbies, professions work well)
  2. Create 10 designs per niche using AI (30 total to start)
  3. Upload to at least 2 platforms
  4. Write keyword-rich titles and descriptions (AI helps here too)
  5. Track which designs get views and sales — double down on winners

Realistic Numbers

With 100 designs uploaded, expect $100-500/month in passive income. Top sellers with 500+ designs report $2,000-5,000/month. The key is volume and niche targeting.

Design Workflows That Sell

Not all AI designs sell equally. Here's the workflow for creating designs that people actually buy.

The Research-First Approach

  1. Trend research: Check bestseller lists on Redbubble and Etsy
  2. Keyword research: Use Etsy search suggestions and eRank for demand data
  3. Competition analysis: Study top sellers — what styles, colors, and themes work?
  4. Design creation: Generate AI designs based on research, not random ideas
  5. Iterate: Create 5-10 variations of your best-performing styles

Niche Research Prompt

I'm creating print-on-demand designs for the [NICHE] market. Help me brainstorm: 1. 10 trending themes or sayings popular with [TARGET AUDIENCE] 2. 5 design style ideas that work well on t-shirts and mugs 3. Seasonal or event-based designs I should create for the next 3 months 4. Keywords that buyers in this niche typically search for Focus on designs that are simple, bold, and readable at a glance. Nothing too complex for print-on-demand.

Marketplace Optimization

Getting designs on the platform is step one. Getting them found is step two. SEO and listing optimization are what separate $50/month sellers from $2,000/month sellers.

Listing Optimization Checklist

  • Title: Front-load the most important keyword. Include niche + product type
  • Tags: Use all available tag slots. Mix broad and specific terms
  • Description: Write for humans and search engines. Include use cases and gift occasions
  • Mockups: Use high-quality product mockups showing designs in context

Listing Description Prompt

Write an Etsy/Redbubble product listing for a [PRODUCT TYPE] with a [DESIGN DESCRIPTION]. Target audience: [WHO] Include: A compelling title (under 140 chars), a description covering who it's perfect for, gift occasions, quality details, and a call-to-action. Also generate 13 SEO tags that buyers would search for. Tone: Fun, friendly, and enthusiastic. Make the buyer visualize owning this product.

Scaling Your Design Business

Once you've found designs that sell, it's time to scale. Here's the playbook for going from side income to serious revenue.

The Scaling Framework

  1. Double down on winners: Create 10-20 variations of your best sellers
  2. Expand platforms: Every design should be on 3+ platforms
  3. Seasonal calendar: Plan designs 4-6 weeks ahead of holidays and events
  4. Batch production: Dedicate one day/week to creating 20+ designs
  5. Automate listings: Use tools to cross-post designs across platforms

Scaling Milestone

The magic number is 500 designs across multiple platforms. At that volume, you'll have enough data to know exactly what sells in your niche, and passive income becomes meaningful.

Finding Micro-SaaS Ideas

Micro-SaaS is a small software product that solves one specific problem for a defined audience. With AI coding tools, non-programmers can now build these.

Where to Find Ideas

  • Tool integrations: Connect two popular tools that don't talk to each other
  • Niche calculators: Build specialized calculators for specific industries

Idea Validation Prompt

I have a micro-SaaS idea: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEA] Help me validate this by analyzing: 1. Who is the specific target user? (job title, industry, company size) 2. What pain point does this solve? How are they solving it now? 3. Are there existing competitors? What would make this different? 4. What's a realistic price point? ($5-50/month for micro-SaaS) 5. Where do these target users hang out online? (for marketing) 6. What's the minimum feature set for a first version? Be honest — if this idea has obvious flaws, tell me what they are.

Info Product Ideation & Validation

Digital info products — ebooks, templates, courses, and guides — have near-zero production costs and infinite margins. AI makes creating them faster than ever.

High-Demand Info Product Types

  • Notion templates: $5-50 each, low effort, high demand
  • Ebooks/guides: $9-49, great for establishing authority
  • Prompt packs: $9-29, sell curated AI prompts for specific use cases
  • Spreadsheet templates: $10-75, solve specific business calculations

Validation Before Creation

Don't spend weeks creating something nobody wants. Validate first:

  3. Create a simple landing page and see if people click "Buy"
  4. Pre-sell to 5 people at a discount before building the full product

AI-Powered Content Creation

Once you've validated your idea, AI can help you create the product in a fraction of the time.

Ebook Creation Workflow

  1. Outline (Day 1): Use AI to brainstorm and structure your table of contents
  2. Draft (Days 2-4): Write chapter by chapter using AI for first drafts
  3. Edit (Days 5-6): Rewrite in your voice, add personal stories and examples
  4. Design (Day 7): Format in Canva or Google Docs, add visuals

Chapter Draft Prompt

I'm writing an ebook about [TOPIC] for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Write Chapter [N]: "[CHAPTER TITLE]" This chapter should cover: - [KEY POINT 1] - [KEY POINT 2] - [KEY POINT 3] Requirements: - 1,500-2,000 words - Include 2-3 practical examples or case studies - Add actionable steps the reader can take immediately - Use a conversational, encouraging tone - End with a transition to the next chapter about [NEXT TOPIC]

Quality Control

AI-generated ebooks that read like AI get refunded. Always rewrite at least 40% of the content, add personal experiences, and include original insights that AI can't generate.

Sales Funnels & Marketing

A great product with no marketing makes $0. Here's how to set up a sales system that works while you sleep.

The Simple Sales Funnel

  1. Free value: Share helpful content on social media or a blog
  2. Lead magnet: Offer a free sample, checklist, or mini-guide
  3. Email sequence: 3-5 emails that build trust and show your expertise
  4. Sales page: Clear benefits, social proof, and a strong CTA
  5. Upsell: Offer a premium version or bundle after purchase

Email Sequence Prompt

Create a 5-email welcome sequence for people who downloaded my free [LEAD MAGNET] about [TOPIC]. The goal is to nurture them toward buying my [PRODUCT] ($[PRICE]). Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the lead magnet + introduce yourself Email 2 (Day 2): Share a quick win or tip related to [TOPIC] Email 3 (Day 4): Tell a story about how you/someone solved [PROBLEM] Email 4 (Day 6): Address the #1 objection: [OBJECTION] Email 5 (Day 8): Make the offer with a deadline or bonus Tone: Friendly, helpful, no hard sell until Email 5. Each email should be 150-250 words.

Passive Income Systems

True passive income requires upfront work, but once the systems are in place, you earn while you sleep. Here's how to build those systems.

The Passive Income Stack

  • SEO content: Write blog posts that rank and drive traffic to your products for months/years
  • Evergreen products: Create products that don't require updates (templates, tools)
  • Automated email funnels: Set up once, convert visitors on autopilot
  • Platform traffic: List on Gumroad, Etsy, and marketplaces that bring buyers to you
  • Affiliate partnerships: Let others promote your product for a commission

Passive Income Reality Check

"Passive" doesn't mean zero effort. It means front-loaded effort. Expect to spend 40-80 hours building a passive income system. Then 2-4 hours/week maintaining it. The payoff: income that continues whether you work this week or not.

Pricing & Packaging Your Services

The biggest mistake new agency owners make is charging too little. Price based on value delivered, not hours spent.

Three Pricing Models

ModelBest ForTypical Range
Project-basedOne-time setups and builds$500 - $5,000
Monthly retainerOngoing management and optimization$500 - $2,000/mo
Value-basedWhen you can prove ROI clearly10-20% of value created

Example Packages

  • Starter ($500): AI chatbot setup on their website with 50 pre-written responses
  • Growth ($1,500): Content automation pipeline + email sequence setup
  • Enterprise ($3,000-5,000): Full workflow audit + 3 automation implementations

Pricing Psychology

Always present three options. Most people choose the middle one. Put your ideal offer in the middle, a basic option below, and a premium option above. This is called "anchoring."
